Bolt Setup

Description: Standard screw bolts are formed via cold heading, an upsetting process performed at the end of a round rod. Using plain carbon steel at 20°C, a mechanical press deforms the billet. The simulation uses finite element analysis to monitor force distribution. The video highlights the upper/lower dies and the transition from initial to final billet shapes.

Bolt with Strain

Description: This simulation tracks strain variation during heading. Strain rises from zero to 3.36107, indicating significant deformation that results in grain refinement and superior mechanical properties. A graph plotting pilot height versus punch force is provided on the bottom left for technical analysis.

Hexagonal Bolt Setup

Description: Hexagonal bolt formation utilizes cold heading at 20°C. The round blank is upset using a mechanical press. The interface displays the processing stages, showing the punch (upper die) and lower die. Finite element division allows for a precise analysis of how the hexagonal head geometry is achieved.

Hexagonal Bolt with Strain

Description: Strain rises to 3.21625 during this operation. The simulation shows the variation of strain across the mesh, particularly localized at the head-to-shank transition. This deformation ensures the hexagonal head possesses the necessary structural integrity for fastening applications.

Screw Head Setup

Description: While typically a multi-step process, this simulation demonstrates screw head formation in a single step for ease of analysis. The plain carbon steel billet undergoes cold heading to create a larger cross-section. The setup highlights the mechanical press interaction with the dies to form the specific screw head profile.

Screw Head with Strain

Description: The strain reaches 3.36107 as the screw head is formed. High strain values correlate with improved hardness and strength through work hardening. The accompanying graph illustrates the relationship between pilot height and the movement of the upper die (punch).
